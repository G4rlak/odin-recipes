Shrimp Scampi

Shrimp Scampi is a classic Italian-American dish featuring succulent shrimp sautéed in a fragrant garlic and butter sauce, then tossed with lemon juice and white wine.

Served over a bed of linguine or with crusty bread, this elegant yet simple dish is perfect for a quick weeknight meal or a special occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup white wine (such as Sauvignon Blanc)
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice (about 2 lemons)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 12 oz linguine (or your preferred pasta)
  • Lemon slices for garnish (optional)

Steps:

  1. Cook pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add linguine and cook according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare shrimp: Season the shrimp with salt and black pepper.
  3. Cook shrimp: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Add shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until pink and opaque. Remove shrimp from the skillet and set aside.
  4. Make sauce: In the same skillet, add minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant. Pour in the white wine and lemon juice, bringing the mixture to a simmer. Cook for 2-3 minutes, allowing the liquid to reduce slightly.
  5. Finish sauce: Reduce heat to low and stir in the butter until melted and the sauce is smooth. Add red pepper flakes if using. Return the cooked shrimp to the skillet, tossing to coat in the sauce. Cook for another 1-2 minutes until heated through.
  6. Combine pasta and sauce: Add the cooked linguine to the skillet and toss to combine with the shrimp and sauce.
  7. Serve: Garnish with fresh parsley and lemon slices if desired. Serve immediately.
Enjoy your delicious Shrimp Scampi!
